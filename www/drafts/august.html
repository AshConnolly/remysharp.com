(edit)

August had come early for me. I was struggling by mid July. I felt sad a lot of the time, and like I was on a tightrope where if my focus shifted off at all, everything would come crumbling around me.

I also hadn't visited Tia's graveside in nearly two months - which we normally do monthly and it helps to release some of the tension.

In the end, last week, I was able to take an afternoon to myself. I visited her grave and when I returned home, read through a diary I kept following the days of her birth and death.

After Tia was born, I needed to write. So I wrote a full account of what happened. Blow by blow. I've only re-read it a handful of times in the last 5 years.

When I did, I cried, hard. The kind of crying that hurts. Like I was squeezing the pain out of my body. The kind of crying that, even that evening, hours later, my face was still worn out.

It helped me.

I find it hard to tell people that she was stillborn. I feel like it does her a disservice. That she's somehow less important. My wife carried Tia to full term. Tia came through natural child birth, and she was still alive the day before - we know because we were in hospital being monitored.

It breaks my heart over and over to think of the time that she was still alive. All I know now is that she's lost. Whenever I think of, or see photos of my wife heavily pregnant, I yearn for a way to change the inevitable outcome.

She is our daughter. She's our first baby. My wife did delivery her. She made no sound coming into the world, and this broke me completely.

The small mercy is that we were able to spend one day with her. To hold her. Lay with her, together, as our family.

It hurts so, so badly that I don't know what colour her eyes are. Still. To this day. 5 years on.

But this is not my darkest memory. The worst thing I've done in my life I cannot change.

After we spent that one single, most precious day with her, we had to leave. I had to leave my poor baby. Alone. Words don't do this justice, but this is the hardest and worst thing I've ever had to do in my life, and I will take that pain to my grave.
