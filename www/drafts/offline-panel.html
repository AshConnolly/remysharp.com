Bandwidth detection

This isn't important, specifically because bandwidth or connection type does not give you the information you want (I've said this before).

Here's why connection type does not matter:

You're tethered to your mobile phone from your laptop. This is using wifi. Your phone is using 3G at best, or has no signal. The laptop still has wifi, and assuming that a wifi connection is good is a road to failure.

Why bandwidth does not matter:

You're at home with super fast (or fast to me) 100Mb connection. Google and the BBC is blazingly fast. But there's a network problem somewhere along the line, and some router is behaving baldy. Or the CPU is particularly high on your server and the net result is that the response time is slow. It doesn't matter if your bandwidth is fat, if the network response is going to be slow, it might as well be a old school modem connection.

An alternative

I think there's two possible alternatives to solve the question of connectivity.

I'm specifically after the connectivity of my server, not of the my machine to the web. So two options I'd be interested in:

  1. Remote host connectivity [async boolean]: simply, does the server answer requests
  2. Ping: this could give us both
